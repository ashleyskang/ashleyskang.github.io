Independent Researcher · Public Health, Neuroscience, Social Cognition

About

My research interests lie at the intersection of public health, neuroscience, and social cognition. I focus on how environmental exposure, trauma, and social interaction shape psychological and population-level outcomes. My work integrates perspectives from neuroscience, psychology, sociocultural analysis, and public health research.

Research Projects

Gun Violence & Public Health Exposure
  • Conducted an independent study examining daily exposure to gun violence, accessibility, and cultural perceptions in the United States.
  • Compared U.S. trends with international contexts through literature review, interviews, and media analysis.
  • Synthesized historical, sociopolitical, and public health perspectives to identify population-level exposure patterns.
Intergenerational Trauma & Brain Development
  • Investigated transgenerational trauma and its effects on brain development, psychological patterns, and mental health outcomes.
  • Conducted interdisciplinary literature review spanning neuroscience, psychology, and sociocultural research.
  • Analyzed relationships between early-life stress exposure, neuroplasticity, and long-term mental health risk.
Neural Synchrony & Social Cognition
  • Explored neural synchrony and its role in social interaction, empathy, and perceptual alignment.
  • Designed interviews and surveys to assess cognitive and perceptual alignment in social settings.
  • Analyzed results using basic statistical methods and compared findings to existing literature on turn-taking, visual cues, and social bonding.
